Performance and student socio-economic background (ESCS index)
Performance and student socio-economic background (ESCS index)
Figure demonstrates a strong relationship at a country level between average student socio-economic background and average science performance. Countries with higher average student socio-economic background perform higher in the PISA science test. More specifically, an increase by one standard deviation in the average student socio-economic background of a country is associated with an increase by around 60 score points in the average science performance. This is less than for reading and mathematics, but it still shows that one standard deviation increase in the PISA meaure of socio-economic background is associated with an increase in country average science performance equivalent to around two years of formal education. This is a substantial difference suggesting that cross-country comparisons should take into account that school systems in different countries work with students of very different socio-economic background.

Figure also shows that some countries perform above and some below the prediction line. China (B-S-J-Z ) shows much better performance than predictions based on their students' socio-economic background, which is at the level below the OECD average. Similarly, students in Taipei, Japan, and Turkey perform much higher than expected based on their socio-economic background. Also, Korea, Poland, and Portugal, stand out showing better performance than countries with a similar level of student socio-economic background.

China and Saudi Arabia have similar levels of the socio-economic background of student families, but performance in Saudi Arabia is much lower. The other countries with similar socio-economic profiles to Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Malaysia, and Chile, perform closer to what is expected. In Saudi Arabia, the average science performance is below the expectation based on the socio-economic background of their students.
