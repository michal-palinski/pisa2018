Gender gap in math achievement
In mathematics, girls in Saudi Arabia perform better than boys, although the gap is much smaller than in reading. In most countries, there is no difference between genders or boys perform better in mathematics, so the result in Saudi Arabia is unusual from an international perspective. The average gender gap in mathematics across the OECD countries is around 5 points in favor of boys. Thus, gender differences are generally much smaller than in reading where girls outperform boys in nearly all countries. There are, however, substantial differences between countries, which show that mathematics instruction and attitudes towards mathematics vary significantly across education systems." %}

As shown in Figure below, among larger education systems that participated in PISA 2018, the gender gaps in mathematics performance are the largest in Thailand, Saudi Arabia, and the Philippines. Girls in these three countries outperform boys by more than ten score points, which is equivalent to less than one year of formal education. Girls perform better also in the Philippines, Indonesia, the United Arab Emirates, Malaysia, and Jordan.
