Chapter summary
This chapter provides detailed overview of key results in science from PISA 2018. Comparisons are provided with the 30 selected countries and for parts of Saudi Arabia.
Percentage of students at each proficiency level in science
The average performance of countries masks significant differences within their education systems. In general, the differences between students and schools in each country are more considerable or comparable to differences between countries. However, in some countries, these disparities are higher while in some smaller. It is why the education systems should be compared not only in terms of average performance but also how unequal are the results of their students. One way of looking at differences between students is to compare how many of them reach international benchmarks, which in PISA are called proficiency levels. In science, PISA 2018 reports results using eight proficiency levels.
Cross country comparison
Average performance in Saudi Arabia is statistically similar to the performance of students in Lebanon and Georgia. Exhibit 5.1. shows that 15-year-olds in Saudi Arabia performed better than their peers in Morocco, Kosovo, Panama, Philippines and Dominican Republic. Student science performance in Saudi Arabia is lower by around 20 score points or less than the performance of students in countries like Peru, Argentina, Brazil, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Baku (Azerbaijan), Kazakhstan and Indonesia. These countries have better results, but the gap with Saudi Arabia students is equivalent to around one year of education or less.
