Performance and student socio-economic background (ESCS index)
Performance and student socio-economic background (ESCS index)
Figure shows that countries like Korea, Taipei, Germany, Portugal, Ukraine, and the United Arab Emirates France show higher than expected performance of the best students, while their actual scores vary. These countries can provide examples of policies that support the best students. On the other hand, they all suffer from the relatively low performance of the weakest students and more substantial inequality.

The opposite relation is found in countries like Mexico, Indonesia, or Morocco, where students at the top perform lower than expected based on the performance of the low-achieving students. However, these countries perform at deficient levels, so overall, their policies do not sufficiently support students at all achievement levels. A better example to follow might be from countries where the performance of students is high and more equalized, like Japan, Poland, and Canada.
