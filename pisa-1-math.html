Chapter summary
This chapter provides detailed overview of key results in math from PISA 2018. Comparisons are provided with the 30 selected countries and for parts of Saudi Arabia.
Percentage of students at each proficiency level in math
The average performance of countries masks significant differences within their education systems. In general, the differences between students and schools in each country are more considerable or comparable to differences between countries. One way of looking at differences between students is to compare how many of them reach international benchmarks, which in PISA are called proficiency levels. In mathematics, PISA 2018 reports results using seven proficiency levels.
Cross country comparison
On average, the mathematics performance of 15-year-old students in Saudi Arabia is 116 score points below the OECD average, which is equivalent to a difference of at least 3 to 4 years of school education. Relatively to reading, the performance of Saudi Arabia students in mathematics places them lower in international rankings. It has to be acknowledged that as well as in reading, among around 80 countries and economies that participated in PISA 2018, almost 50 have the average performance below the OECD average. Thus, although in several countries students perform at a very high level, the majority is close to the OECD average or below. Average performance in Saudi Arabia is statistically similar to the performance of students in Argentina, Indonesia, and Morocco. Figure shows that 15-year-olds in Saudi Arabia performed better than their peers in Kosovo, Panama, Philippines, and the Dominican Republic. Student mathematics performance in Saudi Arabia is lower by around 25 score points or less than the performance of students in countries like Georgia, North Macedonia, Lebanon, Colombia, and Brazil. These countries have better outcomes but close to Saudi Arabia, with the performance difference being equivalent to around one year of education or less.
